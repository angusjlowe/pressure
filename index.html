The Question

  1. Given that Angus weighs 71 kg and the force of gravity on Earth’s surface accelerates everything at 9.81 m/s2, calculate the force acting on Angus due to gravity (Hint: Newton’s 2nd Law).

  2. Angus decides to go snowshoeing up in a town called Barrie to get some much-needed exercise. Each of Angus’s snowshoes are circular for some reason, like an elephant’s foot, and weightless with a diameter of 0.3 m. It is known that the compact snow will collapse when pressure on its surface exceeds 9000 N/m2. Will Angus sink into the snow?

  3. This time, Angus puts on his dad’s snowshoes which are also weightless and circular but have a diameter of 0.6 m. Will Angus sink into the snow?

  4. (Tougher) Angus tries on all kinds of snowshoes of different shapes and sizes. Plot a graph to show the pressure exerted on the snow against the area of the snowshoes and draw a vertical line going through the point at which Angus would sink into the snow. (Hint: Try this website for an idea)
